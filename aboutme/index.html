about


background

I was born in 2001 in Atlanta, Georgia to a Chinese father and a white American mother. My father, who was born and raised in London, immigrated to the United States when he was 21, in 1990. My parents met not long after and had my brother, Timothy, in 1997. My father was naturalized when I was a young child. It is through him that my brother and I have dual nationality between the United States and the United Kingdom. I have resided in Chapel Hill since I was two years old.

education

Educated my entire life in the triangle area of North Carolina, I spent my first two years of high school at Carrboro High School in Carrboro. Following that, I completed my high school diploma and Associate degree at Middle College High School at Durham Technical Community College in Durham. At DTCC I was on the president's list and participated in a research project detailing Chinese American history and literature. I graduated as Saludatorian of MCHS, and currently attend UNC Chapel Hill, studying Advertising and Public Relations in the Hussman School of Journalism and Media. I am also pursuing a minor in studio art, and am part of the Excel@Carolina Accelerated Research Scholars program and Mixed Asian/Pacific Islander Students' Heritage club (MASH).
